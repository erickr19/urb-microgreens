Microgreen FAQs

What are microgreens?

Microgreens can be considered mini-versions of vegetables and herbs.

Examples of this include Brassicaceae such as cauliflower and broccoli.

Space

That mini aspect makes them able to grow in minimal space!

In fact, microgreens can thrive right in your kitchen cabinet with the right set up!

Full of nutrition!

Microgreens contain vitamins, antioxidants, and minerals.

Common between microgreens are vitamin K, vitamin C, and vitamin E.

Harvest times

Microgreens do not require much time to harvest.

They are generally ready to harvest around week two of growth.

Can they regrow?

Microgreens cannot regrow.

However, seeds can be planted right after harvest and use the existing roots as nutrients.

How to enjoy microgreens?

Microgreens can be incorporated into many recipes!

Microgreens can replace or supplement greens. It also can be cooked or even blended in smoothies!

Microgreens and bacteria!

Microgreens themselves cannot get you sick but bacteria introduced in growing conditions can.

Microgreens and Beta-Carotene

Beta-Carotene may reduce the chance of getting eye disease and certain cancers.

Microgreens are full of this mineral and a great substitute for Beta-Carotene containing carrots.
